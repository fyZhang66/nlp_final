Conclusions
Key Takeaways from Our ABSA Pipeline Evaluation
1
Pipeline Works End-to-End
Two-stage ATE → ASC successfully extracts (aspect, sentiment) pairs from raw text. In-domain: 84% ASC accuracy on restaurant, 80% on laptop.
2
ASC Transfers Well
Sentiment classification is robust to domain shift. Cross-domain ASC accuracy: 78-92%, nearly matching in-domain. Sentiment is universal.
3
ATE Is the Bottleneck
Cross-domain ATE drops to 25-36% F1 (vs 52-58% in-domain). ~85% of end-to-end errors originate from ATE failures.
4
Boundary Errors Dominate
~37% of ATE errors are boundary errors (partial spans). Multi-word aspects like "clam chowder" are the hardest challenge.
5
DeBERTa > BERT Consistently
DeBERTa outperforms BERT across all settings. Largest gain: +10.3 pp on cross-domain ATE. Worth the extra compute cost.
6
Neutral Class Is Hard
Neutral F1: 0.51-0.64 across settings (vs 0.88+ for Positive). Class imbalance (~20% Neutral) makes this the weakest class.
Practical takeaway: For real-world ABSA deployment, invest in better ATE (domain-specific training data, joint modeling). ASC can be reused across domains with minimal degradation.
