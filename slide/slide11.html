Key Finding
ATE Degrades Cross-Domain, but ASC Remains Robust
ATE F1 — Degrades Significantly
In-domain range 52 – 58%
Cross-domain range 25 – 36% up to 56% relative drop
ASC Accuracy — Surprisingly Stable
In-domain range 76 – 85%
Cross-domain range 78 – 92% remains high!
Why?
ASC transfers well: [CLS] sentence [SEP] aspect [SEP] captures sentiment patterns that are universal. Words like "great", "terrible", "okay" carry the same sentiment across domains.
ATE is domain-specific: "battery" is an aspect in laptops but not restaurants. "service" is common in restaurants but rare in laptop reviews. Aspect vocabularies don't transfer.
Implication: For real-world cross-domain ABSA, invest in better ATE or domain-specific training data. ASC can reuse existing models.
Anomaly: Lapt→Rest BERT gets 92.2% ASC accuracy on predicted aspects (higher than gold 80.2%!). When ATE only extracts high-confidence easy aspects, ASC gets an easier subset to classify.
