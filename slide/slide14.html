Why DeBERTa Wins
Disentangled attention separates content and position signals, helping the model distinguish tokens in multi-aspect sentences more precisely.
Better boundary detection for multi-word aspects. Relative position encoding helps identify where aspect spans begin and end.
More robust to domain shift in ATE. Biggest gain: Lapt→Rest ATE F1 jumps from 25.8% to 36.1% (+10.3 percentage points).
Trade-off: DeBERTa requires smaller batch sizes (8 vs 16 for ATE) and slightly longer training time due to 26% more parameters. Worth it for the consistent quality gains.
